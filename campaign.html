Established in 1948, the World Health Organization (WHO) stands as a United Nations agency fostering collaboration among nations, partners, and individuals to advance health, enhance global safety, and support the well-being of vulnerable populations. Our mission is to ensure that everyone, regardless of location, can achieve the highest level of health. At the forefront of our initiatives is the leadership in global endeavors to broaden universal health coverage, the coordination of responses to health crises worldwide, and the advocacy for healthier lives, spanning from pregnancy care to elderly well-being. Our ambitious Triple Billion targets articulate a comprehensive strategy for realizing optimal health for all, grounded in evidence-based policies and programs.
