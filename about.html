WHAT IS MENTAL HEALTH?


Mental health denotes a state of well-being that empowers individuals to effectively manage life's challenges, recognize their capabilities, excel in learning and work, and actively contribute to their communities. It serves as a fundamental aspect of overall health, supporting both individual and collective capacities for decision-making, relationship-building, and shaping the world we inhabit. Asserting mental health as a fundamental human right is essential, playing a pivotal role in personal, community, and socio-economic advancement.

Mental health transcends the mere absence of mental disorders; instead, it manifests along a nuanced continuum, varying in experience from person to person. Individuals navigate this continuum with differing levels of difficulty and distress, leading to diverse social and clinical outcomes.

Within the realm of mental health conditions, one encounters not only mental disorders and psychosocial disabilities but also other mental states marked by significant distress, functional impairment, or the potential for self-harm. While individuals with mental health conditions are often prone to lower levels of mental well-being, such an association is not universal or absolute in every case.
